#2: In the woods

On WWDC24, Apple introduced the latest macOS version – Sequoia. How good is it? let's find out!

Best features, as for me:

1. Apple Intelligence. AI promises to make the overall experience better, but I don't have a lot to say. I'm on intel, which, of course, isn't supported, and I will come only this fall in beta, long wait.

2. Notes. This one is big (kinda). Collapsible sections – great! Math notes – great, but more practical on iPad, and near-useless for me until they add Calculus support

3. Window Snapping. A lot of people will say Mac got it too late, but I've got a counter. A window snapping license by IBM (Windows got it thru a partnership) expired recently and didn't let Apple implement the feature earlier.

4. Passwords. Apple, as always, may take a while to implement something. But when it's implemented, it's the best of it's class. Like Passwords, having literally everything you may need, even 2FA built-in! And it's iCloud-supported too!

5. Other features. Tips got an update, iPhone mirroring is on the Mac (I dont have a phone tho...)

What didn't work for me...

1. Apple Intelligence. I'm on an unsupported model, and thus won't experience the new Siri, even the UI >:c. More about this in article #3

2. Wallpaper, kinda... I feel the default wallpaper was made in I colors, but also, it feels a bit unnatural. Very different from the reality; imma change that >:D. More on that in article #4

3. Math Notes. I really love this feature on iPad, however iPads nowadays have giant screens. It would feel way better for that to be on an iPad mini, which I don't have. On mac, it's extremely limited. It's useful for only writing functions without Python.

4. β. Sequoia is still in beta, and still has some bugs. Like websites not loading in Safari, wallpapers not switching to dark mode, etc.

5. Safari Highlights. The great AI summarizing feature available on intel. Works flawlessly, when it works. IRL, worked just for Wikipedia, a plain-texed page with not a lot of text. Hope this will get fixed, please apple!
